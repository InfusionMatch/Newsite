Frequently Asked Questions – For Nurses

What does it mean to be an independent contractor?
As an independent contractor (1099), you are responsible for your own income taxes and liability insurance. Contact your local tax professional for more details.
Why do I need liability insurance?
As a 1099 contractor, liability insurance is a standard practice in our industry, and helps protect you from legal and financial risks.
Is there a minimum or maximum number of shifts that I need to pick up?
You can pick up as many or as few shifts as you want.
Will I know the pay rate before agreeing to a job?
Our custom platform shows you the upfront pay rate before you even apply for a job.
Do you pay for mileage or travel time?
As a 1099 private contractor, and in line with industry standards, mileage and travel time are not compensated. However, you may be able to claim them as tax‑deductible expenses. Consult with your tax professional for more information.
Do I need my own equipment?
You'll need a blood‑pressure cuff, pulse‑oximeter (SpO₂), thermomotor, and stethoscope.
